A Blueprint for Language Native World Models

Niel Ok
Stanford University · April 2025

TL;DR: We introduce a theoretical framework for language-native world modeling that shifts the focus from next-token prediction to structured prediction over latent world states derived from natural language. Our architecture decouples semantic grounding, latent dynamics modeling, and goal verification—enabling agents to simulate and plan within structured latent spaces, not token streams. By extending Shannon entropy to trajectories of semantic states, we treat reasoning as predictive compression over evolving environments.

This framework allows large language models to act as semantic environment simulators—mapping natural language descriptions to internal latent worlds, simulating their evolution, and selecting goal-consistent futures. Unlike conventional prompting pipelines or grounded simulators, our system performs planning and reasoning directly in latent space, supporting modular and interpretable cognition from language alone.

To the best of our knowledge, this is the first architectural blueprint for building language-conditioned agents that reason and model environments through latent semantic simulation.
