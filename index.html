A Blueprint for Language Native World Models

Niel Ok
Stanford University · April 2025

TL;DR: We present a theoretical framework for language-native world modeling in which language models are used purely as semantic translators—mapping natural language descriptions into structured latent representations of world states. These latent states serve as the basis for simulation, planning, and reasoning, fully decoupled from token-level generation.

Our architecture introduces a modular separation between (1) semantic grounding via language-to-latent translation, (2) latent dynamics modeling over projected environment states, and (3) goal verification over latent trajectories. This design enables agents to simulate hypothetical futures entirely within a compressed semantic space, treating reasoning as predictive compression over structured world-state evolution.

To our knowledge, this is the first architectural blueprint for building agents that simulate and plan within latent representations of environments structured from language—not by generating text, but by translating it into dynamic semantic models of the world.
